Virtual Reminiscence

My inspiration for this Virtual Reminiscence Project came from my own
personal experience of growing up in a rural area with my grandparents,
and later moving to the city with my parents. As a child, I was curious about
some of the old and worn-out items in our house, such as a box full of
fabric scraps. My grandmother told me that they had saved these small
pieces of fabric because they were poor and couldn't afford to buy a whole
inch of fabric. But now, most people no longer need to save every little piece
of fabric. I asked my grandmother why she kept the box full of scraps if they
were no longer useful? My grandmother replied by saying that they might
come in handy someday. At that point, my mother said that the patches on
my socks were made from scraps stored in that box. This childhood memory
inspired me to think about why people still hold on to useless items in our
modern society, despite the advanced developments we have today.
I am interested in the nostalgia that is attached to older objects. With that in
mind, I started collecting other peoples' stories that they held onto
alongside their personal items that are from the past.

In the city, I found that there are some elderly people who still live their everyday
lives as if they are still a part of this  past version of life. They kept  some older
habits and lifestyles, such as repairing and continuing to use things even after
they had become worn out. For example, when I asked my parents for a new
television, after it stopped working, they rejected the suggestion and said that
they would try to fix it on their own.  This behavior is not understood by young
people and does not align with the modern concept of life. In modern society, it
is understood that older things should be thrown away and new ones are
bought. For instance,  everyone likes to buy new things, such as  cell phones
and computers. This phenomenon can also be partly attributed to the fact that
older people are neglected by modern society and are unaware of other 
convenient ways of living due to this neglect.

Therefore, I also want to reflect on the conflict between the two ideologies and
different lifestyles in my work by bringing awareness to this phenomenon and
help the audience understand why t the elderly still choose to live in these old
ways while being  in contemporary society. As a young person, I've noticed that
our generation also experiences nostalgia.  Rather than keeping old items in our
homes the way older people do, we choose to preserve memories in a different 
way that is done with our computers and/or cell phones. 
For example, I kept a picture of a doll on my computer that  was a gift from my
grandfather on my 13th birthday. The doll has been discarded, but through this
photo, I can still feel the emotional connection to it.
During the project, I started to think about how I would reflect all of these ideas
at the same time: the creation of nostalgia, the contrast between the past 
lifestyle with the present lifestyle,  and the changes that technology has brought
to these rural and city based lives. 

I tried to use new techniques to deal with old objects.  After a series of trials
and investigations, I finally chose a cyberpunk as the project’s  representation.
I chose this because cyberpunk was  a good way to showcase the conflict
between high-tech and traditional, outdated ways of life that were compelling.
I chose to create a webpage in the form of a game that allows viewers to
explore the items and their stories on their own, while also allowing the users to
experience a sense of virtual reality. The game allows users to search for
objects, click on street pictures to enter different rooms, and view collections of
objects. Through this interactive experience, I hope to evoke emotions related to
nostalgia and our attachment to objects, highlight the differences in our ways of
life based on age and location, and how technology has impacted this.
