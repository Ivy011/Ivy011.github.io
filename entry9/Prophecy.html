Contrived object 27 – Dr Yamaha Electronic components, varnish, copper wires, tennis racket, 68 × 22.5 × 3.5 cm, 2020 This new series of ‘Contrived Object’ is just nothing but faces contrived with what I had in my studio these days of lockdown. Sometimes by just looking at an object, the unconscious mind knows the way the object would like to be transformed. The egg shape of the ‘head’ of these vintage rackets reminded me of something yet familiar but at the moment lost. The result is a composition that resembles vaguely a human face made from a recycled object from the past, the racket, clashing with the rest of the elements, electronic parts, and the found objects. Then, an anomaly called ‘pareidolia’, the mechanism that leads our brain to bring things and objects of all kinds back to known and sensible forms does the rest. Will these be the faces of the future?

Technological mandala 91 – Come in alto così in basso (As above so below) Electronic components, copper wire, stainless steel frame, springs, microchips, speackers, 195cm x 83 cm x 4,5 cm. 2016.

Technology has brought advanced computers, convenient tablets and inseparable cell phones to people's lives, but these products are constantly being upgraded and replaced. What do people do with these obsolete products? Artist Leonardo Ulian soldered electronic components of different shapes and colors together to create intricate and neat designs. This was conceived as a contemporary mandala - a mystical object to be worshipped. The creation is carried out. The links between the components are like neurons in our brain, transmitting and linking information at the speed of light, which in turn constitutes our consciousness - and the universe in the Buddhist worldview. To the artist, these electronic components have not only changed our lives but also the way we see the world; they are symbols of the age.
