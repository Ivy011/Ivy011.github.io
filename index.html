Project 1 On beginning

Click on title to see the code :D

The article I have chosen is on beginning. The main idea of it was that life is a circle, and the end is also a new beginning. After reading it, I thought about my experience of leaving home and studying alone in a foreign country. I buried my deep thoughts about my family in my heart and used it as strength to support me along the way.

For the first two scenes, I wanted to convey a feeling that No matter how I try to hide it, I am still filled with thoughts of my family. I thought that my homesickness would be forgotten with the passage of time, but on the contrary, it only grew inside me like a stream of water.The third scene is about how my life looks very wonderful on the surface, but the feeling is fragile and unreal.

The second stage is about the process of finding myself. The little good things in life made me find myself again, such as finding coins in clothes, reading a meaningful article, and caring words from a stranger. These small warm things put me back together, dried the tears, and got me back on the road.

The third stage is about the power of life. Life is as resilient as a seed. Even under a thick wall, the seed will grow silently in the darkness and eventually break the hardship and bloom a beautiful flower.

In the end, I returned to the article itself, "On Beginning", which I think represents the meaning behind not ignoring difficulties but being hopeful after understanding the struggles of life. I mimicked the end of the movie with the continuous playing of the subtitles, which indicated the end of the story. But its continuous scrolling behavior is meant to imply that it was both an end and a new beginning.

Title: Project 2 Virtual Reminiscence

Click on title to see the code :D

My inspiration for this project came from my own experience of growing up in Shane Xi with my grandparents, and later moving to the city, (Beijing), with my parents. As a child, I was curious about some of the old and worn-out items in our house, such as a box full of fabric scraps. Despite technological advancements, I am interested in the nostalgia that is attached to older objects from an elder’s perspective and from my perspective as a young person in this current society. I started collecting stories on Chinese people (who were between the ages of five years old and 70 years old) that held onto their personal items that were from the past.

In modern Shane Xi, it is understood that older things should be replaced with something new which causes the behavior to be misunderstood by young people. For instance, many of the youth in the city like to buy new things, such as cell phones and computers.

After a series of trials, I chose cyberpunk as the representation for the project because I thought that it was a good way to showcase the conflict between Shan Xi and Beijing’s high-tech development and traditional, outdated ways of life that I thought were compelling. I chose to create a webpage in the form of a game that allows viewers to explore the items and their stories on their own, while also allowing the users to experience a sense of virtual reality.

Title: Project 3 Floral Parks NYC

Click on title to see the code :D

For this project, we were asked to visualize open data about New York City without using representative images or maps. While clearly presenting the data, we also had to develop an idea based on the data. I chose data on the size, location, and addresses of the present parks in New York City. From there, I combined information on New York City itself, and its parks on the website. Then, with all of this information, I researched the flowers that were common in NYC each month and how they were grown in the parks.

While on the website, viewers can click on each of the 12 months as a way to learn about the most common flowers in New York City.  They will then see which parks have that type of flower growing in its park, and the size of the park. I designed the flower shapes to match the shape and color of the actual flowers and as a way mimic the natural growth of the flowers. I also replaced the background images with the four seasons as a way  to correspond the months to the seasons.

At the bottom left of the screen is the name of the flower, and if you hover over the flower’s name you will see a brief description of the flower. Each flower represents a park, and the location of the flower on the screen corresponds to its location on Google Maps. When the viewer clicks on the flower in the screen, the park information will appear. I differentiated the size of the gardens according to large and small, 1-10 acres for small gardens, 10-60 for medium gardens, and 60+ for large gardens. I hope that through this website, audiences will know the size of the garden, where it is located on the map, and how common flowers grow in the park each month in New York City.

Lastly, I designed a small function that when the mouse is detected to stop for 5 seconds without moving, the name of the item will appear on the screen. This way the viewer can remember the name and purpose of the site.

Floral Parks NYC (Key Press+Ardurino)

Click on title to see the code :D

I chose to create visual data on the size, location, and addresses of the parks in New York City, while also researching common flowers found in the city, and how they were grown in these parks.

While on the website that I created, by clicking on different months, viewers can learn about this topic. They will then see which parks have that type of flower growing in it and the park's sizes. I designed the flower shapes and colors to match the different flower types. Then I added animation to mimic the natural growth of the flowers. I replaced the background images with the four seasons as a way to correspond the months to the seasons.

Each flower represents a park, and the location of the flower on the screen corresponds to its location on Google Maps. I hope that through this project, audiences will know the size of the garden, where it is located on the map, and how common flowers grow in the park each month in New York City.

When the keyboard key is pressed, the led lights up according to the color of the flower. For example, a represents January and the color of the flower in January is white, the white led light will light up and the month of January will be displayed on the screen at the same time. Press s to sort the parks by size, A is for all parks, then the micro servo will go to the All position. B is for parks less than 10 acres, then the micro servo will go to the less than 10 position.
